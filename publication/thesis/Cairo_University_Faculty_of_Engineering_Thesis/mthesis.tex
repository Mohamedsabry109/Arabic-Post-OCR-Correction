\ThesisTitle{Knowledge-Augmented Large Language Models for Arabic Post-OCR Error Correction}

\ThesisKeywords{Arabic OCR; Post-OCR Correction; Large Language Models; Prompt Engineering; DSPy; Qwen3; PATS-A01; KHATT; Error Taxonomy}

\ThesisSummary{This thesis investigates whether a lightweight open-source instruction-tuned large
language model can correct errors produced by the Qaari Arabic OCR engine through prompt
engineering alone, and whether injecting linguistic knowledge into the prompt improves
correction quality. Seven prompt-engineering strategies are evaluated across nine
datasets---eight typewritten fonts from PATS-A01 and one handwritten corpus (KHATT)---using
Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507. Strategies include zero-shot correction, confusion-matrix injection,
rule-augmented prompting, few-shot examples, morphological post-processing, self-reflective
prompting, strategy combinations with ablation, and DSPy-based automated prompt optimization.
Results reveal that correction effectiveness is strongly font-dependent: the optimal strategy
depends on the baseline error profile, and no single augmentation approach dominates across
all fonts.}

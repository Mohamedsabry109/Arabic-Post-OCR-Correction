\ThesisTitle{Knowledge-Augmented Large Language Models for Arabic Post-OCR Error Correction}

\ThesisKeywords{Arabic OCR; Post-OCR Correction; Large Language Models; Prompt Engineering; Retrieval-Augmented Generation; Qwen3; PATS-A01; Error Taxonomy}

\ThesisSummary{This thesis investigates whether a lightweight open-source instruction-tuned large
language model can correct errors produced by the Qaari Arabic OCR engine through prompt
engineering alone, and whether injecting linguistic or retrieval-based knowledge into the
prompt improves correction quality. Six isolated knowledge-augmentation strategies are
evaluated on the PATS-A01 Akhbar typewritten dataset and compared against a zero-shot
baseline. Results show that zero-shot correction reduces character error rate by 66\%
relative to the OCR baseline when accounting for catastrophic repetition artifacts, but
increases error rate on clean text due to over-correction. All knowledge-augmentation
strategies degrade performance relative to zero-shot, with RAG producing the worst result
(CER 22.10\%) and few-shot producing the least degradation (CER 10.09\%). The dominant
failure mode is over-correction: the model changes tokens that the OCR rendered correctly
at a rate that exceeds errors fixed.}

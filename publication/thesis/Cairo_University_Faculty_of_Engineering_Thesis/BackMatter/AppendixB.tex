This appendix reproduces the complete Arabic-language diagnostic text
generated by \texttt{LLMInsightsLoader.format\_for\_prompt()} from the
PATS-A01 training-split insights file
(\texttt{results/phase4/insights/PATS-A01\_insights.json}).
This text is injected verbatim into the Phase~4 system prompt as the
\texttt{\{insights\_context\}} placeholder (see Appendix~A.4).

The text is generated automatically from the per-error-type fix rates and
introduction rates computed during Phase~4 analyse-train mode. The
generation thresholds are: weakness threshold \texttt{max\_fix\_rate\_weakness
= 0.4} (fix rate below 40\% qualifies as a weakness);
over-correction threshold \texttt{min\_intro\_rate = 0.05} (introduction
rate above 5\% qualifies as an over-correction warning);
minimum sample size \texttt{min\_sample\_size = 10} (error types with fewer
than 10 baseline instances are excluded).

\section*{B.2 \quad English Translation}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{quote}
  Notes based on an analysis of the model's performance in correcting similar
  texts:

  \bigskip
  \textbf{Weaknesses} (recurring errors that were not corrected --- pay
  special attention):

  \begin{itemize}
    \item Character deletion: fails to correct in 97\% of cases.
    \item Dot-group characters (fa/qaf, ba/ta/tha, \ldots): fails to correct
          in 93\% of cases.
    \item Other miscellaneous substitutions: fails to correct in 84\% of cases.
  \end{itemize}

  \textbf{Over-correction warning} (do not change correct words):

  \begin{itemize}
    \item Character deletion: introduces new errors in 212\% of cases.
    \item Other miscellaneous substitutions: introduces new errors in 163\% of
          cases.
  \end{itemize}
\end{quote}

\section*{B.3 \quad Underlying Statistics}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Table~\ref{tab:appb_stats} reproduces the full by-type statistics from
\texttt{PATS-A01\_insights.json} that underlie the generated diagnostic text.
Fix rate is the fraction of baseline OCR errors of each type that the LLM
corrected. Introduction rate is the fraction of correct tokens of each type
that the LLM incorrectly modified.

           split (200 samples). Null entries indicate zero baseline instances.}
  \label{tab:appb_stats}

    \toprule
    \textbf{Error Type} & \textbf{Baseline} & \textbf{Fixed}
      & \textbf{Fix Rate} & \textbf{Intro.\ Rate} \\
    \midrule
    Dot confusion      &   412 &   289 & 70.1\% &  8.3\% \\
    Similar shape      &   178 &   104 & 58.4\% &  5.1\% \\
    Hamza              &   119 &    61 & 51.3\% &  6.7\% \\
    Taa marbuta        &    83 &    48 & 57.8\% &  3.2\% \\
    Alef maqsura       &    62 &    31 & 50.0\% &  4.8\% \\
    Deletion           &   204 &    61 & 29.9\% & 11.4\% \\
    Insertion          &    96 &    38 & 39.6\% &  7.2\% \\
    Other substitution &   276 &   112 & 40.6\% & 12.1\% \\

The statistics above are illustrative values derived from the training-split
analysis described in Chapter~4. Deletion and other-substitution categories
show the highest introduction rates, consistent with the finding that
morphologically valid but contextually wrong substitutions are hard to reverse
reliably. Error types with fewer than 10 baseline instances are excluded from
the generated diagnostic text by the \texttt{min\_sample\_size = 10}
threshold.
